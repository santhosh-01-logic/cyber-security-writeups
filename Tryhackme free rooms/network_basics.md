TryHackMe - Networking Basics

Today I learned the basics of networking.

A network is a group of two or more devices connected together so they can communicate and share data.

Devices can be connected in different ways:

- Wired connection using cables such as Ethernet.
- Wireless connection using technologies such as Wi-Fi.

Both methods allow devices to exchange information across a network.

Private Network

A private network is used within a home, school, or organization.

Devices inside the network can communicate with each other, and access is usually restricted to authorized users.

Public Network and the Internet

The Internet is a global network that connects millions of smaller networks around the world.

When devices are connected through the Internet, they can communicate with systems located anywhere in the world.

History of the Internet

One of the earliest computer networks was ARPANET.

ARPANET was developed by the U.S. Department of Defense and became the foundation for the modern Internet.

Later, Tim Berners-Lee created the World Wide Web (WWW).

The Internet and the World Wide Web are not the same thing:

- The Internet is the global network infrastructure.
- The World Wide Web is a service that runs on top of the Internet and allows us to access websites through web browsers.

Key Takeaways

- A network consists of connected devices that communicate with each other.
- Devices can connect using wired or wireless technologies.
- Private networks are used internally by homes and organizations.
- The Internet connects networks across the world.
- ARPANET was the foundation of the Internet.
- The World Wide Web was invented by Tim Berners-Lee and made websites possible.
